Why Discovery
Changes Everything
Most reseller conversations start and end with the customer's stated need. The best ones go somewhere else entirely.
// The Problem
Your customer walks in with an order. Do you take it?
A customer calls and says "we need colocation space." The natural instinct is to respond with specs, pricing, and availability. You get the order. You move on. This is how most reseller conversations work — and it's exactly what separates average partners from elite ones.
Taking the order feels efficient. But it leaves enormous value on the table — for the customer, for the deal, and for the relationship. When you only solve the stated problem, you miss the real problem. And in enterprise infrastructure, the real problem is almost always bigger, more urgent, and more worth solving.
"We don't just sell space. We orchestrate outcomes."
— Equinix sales philosophy

// The Framework
The EDGE Sales Methodology
Equinix's workload-led approach is built around a four-stage framework. Discovery is not a single event — it's woven through every customer interaction. The EDGE methodology gives you a structure for making every conversation count.
E
Explore
Understand the customer's business, their goals, and what's driving the conversation right now.
D
Discover
Uncover requirements across all dimensions — workload, data, users, connected systems, and beyond.
G
Design
Architect the right solution — tailored to the customer's actual workflow, not their stated ask.
E
Execute
Deliver with precision. The quality of your discovery determines the quality of your solution.
// What to Discover
The 7 Discovery Dimensions
A complete discovery covers seven dimensions. Each one is a question you need to be able to answer before you can architect the right solution. Every dimension you miss is a gap your AE will have to fill — or a deal that gets smaller than it should be.
DIMENSION 01
"What does it do?"
The Workload — understanding what the system actually does unlocks everything else.
DIMENSION 02
"Why does it matter?"
Business Outcome — connect infrastructure to the business result it enables.
DIMENSION 03
"Who uses it?"
Users — location, volume, and proximity requirements all flow from this answer.
DIMENSION 04
"Where's the data?"
Data Location — where data lives and where it needs to go shapes the entire architecture.
DIMENSION 05
"What must it talk to?"
Connected Systems — dependencies define the interconnection strategy.
DIMENSION 06
"How critical is it?"
Performance — uptime, latency, and availability requirements determine the right design.
DIMENSION 07 // HIDDEN
"What aren't they telling you?"
The Hidden Need — the most valuable discovery is always the one the customer didn't lead with. You have to earn it.
// What Good Looks Like
Workloads drive everything
When you lead with workload, the conversation changes. Instead of talking about space, power, and price — you're talking about AI diagnostics, imaging latency, data sovereignty, and business outcomes. Instead of competing on cost, you're solving problems no one else even identified.
The customer's stated need is always a starting point — never a destination. The best discovery conversations follow a thread: each answer opens a new question, each question surfaces a deeper requirement, until you have a picture the customer couldn't have drawn for themselves.
"You don't need to have all the answers. You just need to ask the right questions."
— Socrates
// Knowledge Check
A reseller's customer opens a call by saying "We need colocation space for our Epic EMR system." What is the best next move?
Correct. The workload-led approach starts with understanding what the system does and why it matters — not with specs or pricing. "What does Epic EMR do for your business?" opens the entire discovery conversation.
Not quite. Jumping to specs or pricing skips the most important part — understanding the workload, the business outcome, and the users. The right first move is to ask about the system itself.

// Key Takeaways
TAKEAWAY 01
Lead with workload, always
Every customer opens with a stated need. The workload behind that need is where the real deal lives. Never let the stated ask define the conversation.
TAKEAWAY 02
The hidden need is the deal
Meridian's data sovereignty issue was the actual buying trigger — and it was worth far more than the original ask. The hidden need is always worth finding.
TAKEAWAY 03
Compliance unlocks conversations
In regulated industries like healthcare, asking about compliance, data residency, and regulations almost always surfaces something important. Make it a habit.
TAKEAWAY 04
Discovery quality = deal size
The more dimensions you uncover, the more complete the solution you can architect — and the larger and more strategic the deal becomes.
